Rocket Drone Aerodynamic Design

Full aerodynamic characterization of a VTOL rocket-drone hybrid across transonic regime

ANSYS Fluent Turbulence Modeling Compressible Flow
+
Problem Statement

Design a rocket-drone hybrid capable of stable flight across Mach 0.3–0.8, minimizing base drag while maintaining structural feasibility. The blunt-body transition zone between fuselage and tail fins presented significant flow separation challenges.

SolverANSYS Fluent — Density-based
Turbulencek-ω SST (Menter)
Mesh TypeUnstructured hybrid, ~4.2M cells
RegimeSubsonic to Transonic (M = 0.3–0.85)
Duration 3 months
Results & Insights
  • 18% reduction in total aerodynamic drag compared to baseline geometry through nose fairing optimization
  • Eliminated flow separation at fin root junction using leading-edge contouring — validated with streamline visualization
  • Achieved stable lift curve slope (CLα) across full AoA sweep (0°–16°) with stall onset at 14.5°
  • Pressure drag coefficient reduced from Cd = 0.48 to Cd = 0.29 at cruise Mach

UAV Wing Aerodynamic Optimization

Multi-objective wing planform optimization for extended endurance UAV platform

OpenFOAM Shape Optimization L/D Ratio
+
Problem Statement

Optimize a fixed-wing UAV (MTOW ~3.5 kg) for maximum endurance at 20 m/s cruise. Baseline wing exhibited early separation near the leading edge at higher AoA, limiting effective operating envelope to ±8° before rapid L/D degradation.

SolverOpenFOAM — simpleFoam (steady RANS)
TurbulenceSpalart-Allmaras
Mesh TypeSnappyHexMesh, ~2.8M cells
Airfoil NACA 4412 → custom modified profile
Results & Insights
  • L/D ratio improved from 14.2 to 19.8 through camber redistribution and tip geometry modification
  • Separation bubble at LE suppressed by 60% via leading-edge radius optimization — operating envelope extended to ±13°
  • Estimated 22% increase in endurance at cruise condition — validated with simplified Breguet range equation
  • Induced drag reduced 15% with winglet addition optimized via ANSYS Fluent adjoint solver

03

Engine Intake Flow Optimization

Internal duct flow optimization for turbofan intake — total pressure recovery maximization

ANSYS Fluent Internal Flow Pressure Recovery
+
Problem Statement

Analyze and improve total pressure recovery of an S-duct intake geometry under crosswind and off-design flight conditions. Secondary flow vortices in the S-bend were causing 12% total pressure loss and non-uniform Mach distribution at the AIP (Aerodynamic Interface Plane).

SolverANSYS Fluent — Pressure-based
Turbulencek-ω SST with curvature correction
MeshStructured hex, ~6.1M cells
RegimeSubsonic internal flow, M = 0.2–0.45
Results & Insights
  • Total pressure recovery improved from 0.88 to 0.96 through vortex generator placement optimization
  • AIP Mach non-uniformity (DC60) reduced by 35% — engine compatibility significantly improved
  • Identified critical separation region at inner S-duct wall — addressed via contour modification and local divergence tuning
  • Flow uniformity index at AIP increased from 0.76 to 0.93
